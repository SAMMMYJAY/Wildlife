The Trouble with Bears

By Evan Wild

Lumbering, angry, dangerous—the real live bears of this world are proud, independent creatures, self-serving and always on the hunt for food.

So what are bears really like, and why does the world's media portray them with such a skewed vision? In this article, we try to answer these questions and give you a real insight into the life of bears.

Types of Bears

Bears come in two varieties—large and medium. You don't get small bears. If you have seen small bears, then it was probably a baby bear (cub) from another species.

LION

- Known as the "king of the jungle"

- Large carnivorous mammal with a short, tawny or golden coat

- Lives in prides, typically consisting of several females, their cubs, and one or more males

- Found in savannas and grasslands of Africa and India

- Known for their majestic manes and powerful roars

DOG

- Domesticated carnivorous mammal, descended from wolves

- Highly social and loyal, often living with human families

- Various breeds, ranging from small (Chihuahua) to large (Great Dane)

- Found worldwide, in diverse environments

- Known for their intelligence, trainability, and affectionate nature

GORILLA

- Largest primate species, divided into two main types: Eastern and Western Gorillas

- Herbivorous, with a diet consisting mainly of leaves, shoots, and stems

- Lives in small groups, led by a dominant male (silverback)

- Found in forests and mountains of Central Africa

- Known for their intelligence, strength, and gentle nature

ELEPHANT

- Largest land animal, with African and Asian species

- Herbivorous, with a diet consisting mainly of grasses, leaves, and fruits

- Lives in large matriarchal herds, led by the oldest female

- Found in savannas, forests, and deserts of Africa and Asia

- Known for their exceptional memory, social behavior, and distinctive trunks

TIGER

- Largest cat species, with six subspecies

- Carnivorous, with a diet consisting mainly of large ungulates

- Found in forests and grasslands of Asia

- Solitary animals, with males occupying large territories

- Known for their distinctive orange and black stripes, and endangered status
